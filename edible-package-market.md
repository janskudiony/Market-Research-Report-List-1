<p><p>The edible packaging market encompasses various types including lipids, polysaccharides, proteins, surfactants, and composite films. Lipids, derived from fats and oils, provide moisture barrier properties. Polysaccharides, such as starches and cellulose, offer flexibility and biodegradability. Proteins, found in natural sources, contribute to mechanical strength and film-forming capabilities. Surfactants enhance the integration of ingredients, improving texture and stability. Composite films combine multiple materials to optimize performance, balancing functionality and sustainability in food packaging applications.</p></p>
