<p><ul><li>Wet Friction Clutch</li><li>Dry Friction Clutch</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The automotive friction clutch market is divided into two main types: wet friction clutches and dry friction clutches. Wet friction clutches operate in a lubricated environment, allowing for smoother engagement and reduced wear, making them common in high-performance and automatic transmission vehicles. In contrast, dry friction clutches utilize no lubrication, resulting in simpler designs and lower manufacturing costs, often found in manual transmission vehicles. Each type serves distinct applications, balancing performance, durability, and operational efficiency.</p></p>
