<p><ul><li>Resistant</li><li>Superconductors</li><li>Hybrids</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The electromagnet market is categorized into three types: resistant, superconductors, and hybrids. Resistant electromagnets use traditional materials to generate magnetic fields, often facing limitations due to thermal losses. Superconductors, on the other hand, utilize materials that exhibit zero resistance at low temperatures, enabling powerful magnetic fields without energy loss. Hybrid electromagnets combine both types, leveraging the strengths of resistant and superconducting materials for varied applications. This segmentation helps meet diverse industry needs in areas like transportation, medical imaging, and research.</p></p>
