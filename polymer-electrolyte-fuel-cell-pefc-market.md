<p><p>The Polymer Electrolyte Fuel Cell (PEFC) market primarily focuses on hydrogen storage types: compressed gaseous hydrogen, cryogenic liquid hydrogen, and hydrides. Compressed gaseous hydrogen involves storing hydrogen at high pressures, ensuring efficiency and convenience. Cryogenic liquid hydrogen entails storing hydrogen at extremely low temperatures, offering a high energy density but requiring sophisticated insulation. Hydrides utilize metal alloys to bond with hydrogen, allowing safer and more compact storage. Each type presents unique advantages for fueling PEFC applications across various industries.</p></p>
