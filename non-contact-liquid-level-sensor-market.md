<p><ul><li>Ultrasound</li><li>Capacitive</li><li>Hydrostatic</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The non-contact liquid level sensor market comprises three main types: ultrasound, capacitive, and hydrostatic sensors. Ultrasound sensors use sound waves to detect liquid levels without physical contact, offering high accuracy and versatility. Capacitive sensors measure changes in capacitance caused by the presence of liquid, making them suitable for various fluid types. Hydrostatic sensors gauge the pressure at the bottom of a liquid column, allowing for precise level measurement based on fluid density. Each type serves distinct applications and industries.</p></p>
