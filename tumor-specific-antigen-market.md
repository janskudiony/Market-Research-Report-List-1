<p><p>The Tumor-Specific Antigen (TSA) market comprises two main types: Coding Region and Non-Coding Region antigens. Coding Region antigens arise from mutated protein-coding genes, generating unique proteins that can be targeted by the immune system. In contrast, Non-Coding Region antigens are derived from rare mutations or alterations in non-coding DNA regions that can also trigger immune responses. Both types play crucial roles in cancer immunotherapy, diagnostics, and vaccine development, offering potential for targeted treatment options.</p></p>
